Personal

My interest in programming began with a fascination with video games. I spent a lot of time when I was younger playing flash games on contributor driven sites like Kongregate. Some of the best games were simple and designed by kids no older than me. I enjoyed playing the games and wondered if I could make them. So I began learning Python to get an understanding of the simpler concepts and syntax of programming. From there I learned ActionScript 3 and made a couple simple working games.

In that time I discovered I loved programming. The challenge and discovery of creating something drew me in and I would program and look up syntax for hours at a time. At the time I still had misguided ideas about transitioning to a career in development and thought it was out of reach. After some research I decided that at the very least I wanted to try. I was drawn to Javascript as it could accomplish similar functions with canvas as ActionScript and Flash but with wider support. With many browsers disabling Flash as the default I felt Javascript was a better language to focus on.

I've began learning Javascript and related technologies through books on packt and courses on Free Code Camp. In the last year I was very fortunate to apply and advance through a scholarship with Google on Udacity that has solidified my technical web abilities, allowed me to produce a functioning project, and left me confident that I am ready for a full time position.
